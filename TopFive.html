5 Little Free Libraries of Athens, Ohio


Little Free Libraries dot many communities across the nation- specifically those who recognize the need for free literature that is available to absolutely anyone, anytime.

Below you can find some of my favorite library boxes that can be found in my home town of Athens, Ohio


The Mount Zion Church Library

Mount Zion Baptist Church Preservation Society, 32 W Carpenter St, Athens, OH 45701

Mount Zion Baptist Church Preservation Project : "Mount Zion Baptist Church Preservation Society is a 501(c)3 non-profit organization dedicated to restoring the Mount Zion Baptist Church into a vibrant community center."

This library was nearly empty. It must be a popular spot among curious college readers!


North Congress Street Library

158 N Congress St, Athens, OH 45701

The North Congress Street Free Library is not easy to find. You'll spot it along the side of the sidewalk up a hill, right at the end of a paved driveway surrounded by nice brick walls.

This library was filled to the brim with a few best sellers and a lot of self-help books!


S May Avenue Library

28 S May Ave, Athens, OH 45701

You can find this little library near the Speedway Gas station, hidden comfortably between rose bushes and palm trees in a very well manicured garden.

South May's library has a smaller form-factor than most. I noticed a few pulp fiction books and romance novelas.


Elmood Place Library

85 Elmwood Pl, Athens, OH 45701

This humble little box of books hides on Elmood place, inside the same neighborhood as our last library.

The selection of books here was amazing. A lot of diversity; instructional handbooks, romance, drama. Even some best sellers. If you're looking for a worth while read on a non-existant budget, check here first!


The Farmacy Library

28 W Stimson Ave, Athens, OH 45701

Amid renovations and street work on Stimson Avenue, The Farmacy's little library hides under the awning close to their exit. This box has been relocated, and isn't in the best condition as of October 2021.

As you can see in my images, there are very few books left here. None of them were in the best condition either, and reflects this interstitial state of the library.
